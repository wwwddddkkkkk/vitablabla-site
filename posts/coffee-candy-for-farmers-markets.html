What makes a candy market-friendly.

Not every sweet survives a warm morning on the move. A market-friendly candy tends to share a short list of traits:

  • One-handed: it opens and eats with a single hand while the other holds the bags.
  • Heat-steady: it doesn't melt into a mess in a sunny pocket the way chocolate would.
  • Pocket-sized: small enough to ride in the one pocket a full tote leaves you.
  • Slow to finish: a single piece stretches across several stalls instead of one bite.
  • Tidy: sealed and crumb-free, so it won't sticky up the produce you're carrying.

Seen together, those traits describe a very specific kind of small reset — one designed for being on your feet, outdoors, with your hands committed elsewhere.
