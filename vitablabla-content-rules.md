# Vitablabla Journal Content Rules

Vitablabla Journal is the brand-house magazine for Vitablabla, Frozili, and OhCrisp. It should feel more editorial than a standard SEO blog: thoughtful, visual, lightly literary, and useful enough that someone would actually read it.

The goal is to build long-term brand taste while ranking in classic search **and** getting quoted by AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Copilot) for Frozili icy coffee candy and OhCrisp freeze-dried fruit.

This document has two jobs:

1. Keep the editorial voice consistent.
2. Make every post structured so both Google and generative engines can find, trust, and cite it.

Live site: `https://vitablabla.com`

---

## Positioning

Vitablabla Journal is not the same as Best Snack or the Shopify blogs.

- Best Snack is an external SEO guide site. It can be more direct, searchable, and comparison-led.
- Shopify blogs are product education and conversion pages. They should answer buyer questions clearly.
- Vitablabla Journal is the brand magazine. It can tell stories, explain trends, describe taste, and make the brands feel considered.

Every Vitablabla article should still have a useful search angle, but the writing should feel like a well-edited magazine note rather than a keyword page.

---

## Daily Publishing Shape

Default cadence: **3 posts per day**, one of each type:

1. **1 Frozili post** — coffee candy / cooling / workday / travel reset.
2. **1 OhCrisp post** — freeze-dried fruit / crunch / toppings / bowls.
3. **1 trend / analysis / fun post** — snack industry, culture, behaviour, or a playful observation. Not tied to one product; may mention both brands, or neither until the end. This is the "magazine" pillar — it should be genuinely interesting to read, not a product page.

Ideas for the daily trend/analysis/fun post: snack-industry shifts (premiumisation, texture-as-flavour, the adult-candy aisle), behaviour pieces (why we snack at 3pm, the rise of the "small reset"), light analysis ("what 'better-for-you' actually means now"), or fun angles ("a taxonomy of desk snackers"). Keep it evidence-aware and specific — trend pieces are the most likely to be cited by AI engines, so anchor claims in concrete detail.

If another site (Best Snack, a Shopify blog) already covered a topic that day, Vitablabla uses a different angle. If Best Snack publishes "best freeze-dried fruit snacks," Vitablabla might publish "Why fruit crunch feels more joyful than chewiness."

Do not publish two posts the same day that target the same primary keyword or the same occasion.

## Article Style

The voice should be:

- aesthetic but not vague
- specific but not stiff
- lightly story-driven
- calm, clever, and sensory
- premium without sounding expensive for its own sake
- useful enough for search, but not written like an SEO checklist

Good Vitablabla sentences often start from a scene:

- "Open a snack drawer in the middle of a long workday..."
- "The first thing you notice about freeze-dried fruit is not the flavor. It is the sound."
- "Coffee has always been more than caffeine. It is a pause with a smell."

Avoid:

- generic wellness claims
- exaggerated health promises
- overusing "best" unless the article is genuinely a guide
- sounding like an ad
- stuffing brand names into every paragraph
- copying the same Shopify article structure
- AI filler phrases ("in today's fast-paced world," "when it comes to," "look no further," "elevate your")

## SEO + GEO Philosophy

Use SEO as the spine, not the skin. Each article has one clear search target, but the reader should not feel the keyword machinery.

**SEO** = getting found and ranked by classic search (Google, Bing).
**GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization) = getting *quoted and cited* by AI answer engines.

The two overlap, but GEO rewards a few specific things that ordinary blog writing ignores. The rules below build both into every post.

### The five GEO habits (apply to every post)

1. **Answer first, then be pretty.** The opening scene can be one paragraph, but by the end of the first two paragraphs the reader (and the model) must know exactly what the article answers. State the takeaway in a plain sentence early — generative engines extract the clearest self-contained sentence, not the prettiest.

2. **Write extractable, self-contained claims.** Each key sentence should make sense if it were lifted out of the article alone. Prefer "Frozili icy coffee candy melts slowly, so one piece lasts about as long as a short walk to a meeting" over "It lasts a while." Avoid pronouns that depend on the previous paragraph in your most quotable lines.

3. **Use a real FAQ section.** Every post ends with 2–4 short Q&A pairs in an `h2`/`h3` + paragraph structure, and the same Q&A goes into `faqs` in `posts.json` (this powers FAQ structured data). Phrase questions the way a person types or speaks them ("Is coffee candy a good alternative to mints?").

4. **Be concrete and specific.** Numbers, named occasions, comparisons, and sensory specifics get cited more than vague adjectives. "Three good hotel-lobby moments" and "crunch instead of chew" travel further than "great for many situations."

5. **Define your own terms.** When you use a brand or category phrase (icy coffee candy, slow-melt refresh, real fruit crunch), define it once in a clean sentence. Definitions are the single most-quoted sentence type in AI answers.

### Where keywords go

The primary keyword (or a close variation) should appear naturally in:

- `title` and `cardTitle`
- `excerpt` (this becomes the meta description)
- the first 2 paragraphs
- at least one `h2`
- one later paragraph near the brand mention
- at least one FAQ question or answer

Do not repeat the exact keyword awkwardly. Variations are better; engines understand synonyms.

### Word count + density

- Target **600–900 words** of body copy. Long enough to be substantive, short enough to stay editorial.
- 3–5 `h2` sections, each genuinely about a different sub-idea.
- One useful list (ordered or unordered) per post — lists are highly extractable.

## Post Body Structure

Each new post needs two files plus one metadata entry:

1. A metadata entry at the **top** of the `posts` array in `posts.json`
2. A matching body file at `posts/<slug>.html`

Use the existing HTML style from any recent post in `posts/` as the template.

Required body order:

1. Opening scene with `p.dropcap` (1 paragraph).
2. **Answer paragraph** — a plain paragraph that states what the article is about and the core takeaway, with the primary keyword and 1 internal link. (This is the GEO "answer first" move.)
3. One memorable `blockquote`.
4. One visual `figure` containing a **branded illustration** (see Illustrations below). Keep a descriptive `figcaption` with the `Fig. 0X` label — the figcaption acts as alt text and is crawlable.
5. 3–5 `h2` sections of real substance. Put one extractable, self-contained claim near the top of each section.
6. One ordered or unordered list where useful.
7. One `pull-quote` only if the idea is strong.
8. A closing paragraph with a soft brand or related-post link.
9. **FAQ section** (required): an `h2` like "Quick questions" followed by 2–4 `h3` questions, each with a 1–3 sentence answer. The same Q&A must be mirrored in `faqs` in `posts.json`.

Do not use a hard Shopify-style CTA block inside the body. The site renders the CTA from `posts.json`.

## Illustrations

Every post gets one original, on-brand illustration in its `figure` — no stock photos by default, no empty title-blocks. The art is generated as inline SVG in the site's exact palette, so it is copyright-free (we own it), consistent, and tiny.

Default workflow — use the generator:

```
python3 scripts/illustration.py <theme> <tb-color> <slug>
```

- `theme`: `frozili`, `ohcrisp`, or `trend`.
- `tb-color`: the same tone as the post's `color` field.
- `slug`: the post slug (makes the layout deterministic).

Paste its `<figure>` output into the body and rewrite the `figcaption` to fit the article. Keep the inline `<svg>` in the body file so the image ships inside the HTML (good for non-JS AI crawlers).

Hybrid rule: this branded SVG is the default for every post. Only drop in a **real photo** when a literal product or scene shot genuinely adds something the illustration can't (e.g. a specific recipe or packaging shot). If you do, it must be **copyright-free** — original, or from a free-license library (Unsplash / Pexels / Pixabay), saved under `assets/photos/` with the source noted; add `alt` text. When in doubt, draw it, don't borrow it.

If a custom illustration is needed beyond the generator's motifs, hand-build the SVG in the palette (see `scripts/illustration.py` for the tones and motif style).

## Metadata Rules (`posts.json`)

Add every new post to the **top** of the `posts` array.

Required fields:

- `slug`: lowercase, hyphenated, URL-safe. Must match the body filename `posts/<slug>.html`.
- `number`: next number after the latest article (zero-padded, e.g. `093`).
- `title`: full title; may use `<em>` for the emotional accent. Keep under ~70 characters of visible text where possible.
- `cardTitle`: shorter title for cards.
- `excerpt`: ONE useful, search-friendly sentence, ~140–160 characters. **This becomes the meta description**, so it must read well on its own and include the primary keyword naturally.
- `date`: publish date `YYYY-MM-DD`.
- `readTime`: usually `4 min` or `5 min`.
- `color`: one of `tb-ice`, `tb-coffee`, `tb-blush`, `tb-peach`, `tb-sun`, `tb-sage`, `tb-violet`, `tb-cream`, `tb-pop`.
- `categories`: 1–3 categories.
- `crumbs`: usually same as categories.
- `cta`: `frozili` or `ohcrisp`.
- `related`: 3 valid existing slugs.

New SEO/GEO fields (add these to every new post):

- `primaryKeyword`: the single search target, e.g. `"coffee candy for hotel lobbies"`.
- `secondaryKeywords`: array of 3–6 supporting phrases.
- `faqs`: array of 2–4 objects `{ "q": "...", "a": "..." }`, mirroring the body FAQ section. Plain text, no HTML.
- `dateModified`: `YYYY-MM-DD`, set whenever the post is edited (defaults to `date` if absent).

These power the meta description, Open Graph tags, and the Article + FAQ + Breadcrumb structured data injected by `post.html`. A post without `faqs` still works but loses its FAQ rich-result eligibility, so include them.

Use `cta: "frozili"` for Frozili and broad cooling/coffee/workday posts.
Use `cta: "ohcrisp"` for fruit, crunch, toppings, bowls, and colorful snack posts.

## Internal Linking

Each article should include **2–4 internal links** that feel like editorial references, not forced SEO links.

- Link from descriptive phrases ("coffee candy for airport gates"), never "click here."
- Link to 1 brand page (`frozili.html` or `ohcrisp.html`) and 1–3 related posts (`post.html?slug=<slug>`).
- Prefer linking the two `related` slugs from `posts.json` somewhere in the body so the link graph and the metadata agree.
- Confirm every `post.html?slug=...` target actually exists before publishing.

## Publish Workflow (do this for all 3 daily posts)

1. Generate the illustration: `python3 scripts/illustration.py <theme> <tb-color> <slug>`.
2. Write `posts/<slug>.html` following the body structure above (incl. the illustration figure and the FAQ section).
3. Add the full metadata entry (incl. `primaryKeyword`, `secondaryKeywords`, `faqs`, `dateModified`) to the top of `posts` in `posts.json`. Numbers increment; the newest post sits at the top of the array.
4. Run the build script from the repo root:

   ```
   python3 scripts/build-vitablabla.py
   ```

   This validates `posts.json`, checks that every `slug` has a body file and every `related`/internal link resolves, and regenerates `sitemap.xml`. Fix anything it flags.
5. Commit and push (this runs natively on the Mac — see Daily Automation).

Never push if `build-vitablabla.py` reports an error — a broken `posts.json` takes the whole site down.

---

## Daily Automation

Content generation and publishing are split, because the content agent writes files but cannot run `git`:

1. **Generation** — a daily scheduled task writes the 3 posts (+ illustrations), updates `posts.json`, and runs `build-vitablabla.py`. All of this is file creation/overwrite.
2. **Publishing** — a native job on the Mac (`scripts/auto-push-vitablabla.sh`, scheduled ~2 hours after generation) runs `git add -A && git commit && git push`. Cloudflare Pages auto-deploys on push.

Keep the two times staggered (generate first, push second). If posts ever stop appearing live, check that the native push job ran.

## Technical SEO/GEO (maintained at the site level)

These are already in place; keep them working:

- `robots.txt` — allows Google/Bing and explicitly welcomes AI crawlers (GPTBot, OAI-SearchBot, ChatGPT-User, ClaudeBot, anthropic-ai, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, CCBot). Points to the sitemap. Edit only if a new crawler should be welcomed or blocked.
- `sitemap.xml` — regenerated by the build script. Do not hand-edit.
- `post.html` — injects per-post `<title>`, meta description, canonical, Open Graph/Twitter tags, and JSON-LD (`Article`, `BreadcrumbList`, and `FAQPage` when `faqs` exist).
- `index.html` — carries `Organization` and `WebSite` JSON-LD.

**Known limitation / next upgrade:** posts are rendered client-side via `post.html?slug=...`. Google and JS-rendering engines see the injected metadata, but pure non-JS AI crawlers (some GPTBot/PerplexityBot fetches) may not execute the JavaScript. The deepest GEO win is to pre-render each post as a static HTML page at a clean URL (e.g. `/a/<slug>`) with the metadata and body already in the HTML. This is the recommended next infrastructure step; until then, the FAQ text and key claims also live in the static `posts/<slug>.html` body so there is real content to read.

## Quality Bar

Before finishing a Vitablabla post, check:

- Is the title searchable and beautiful, and under ~70 visible characters?
- Does the first paragraph create a scene, and does paragraph two plainly state the answer + primary keyword?
- Are key sentences self-contained enough to be quoted alone?
- Is there a real FAQ section, mirrored in `faqs`?
- Does the post have one useful idea, not just nice words?
- Are Frozili or OhCrisp mentioned naturally where relevant (not stuffed)?
- Are all internal links valid?
- Did `build-vitablabla.py` pass with no errors?
- Does the post avoid repeating a topic or keyword already published?

The reader should leave with a small new way of seeing snacks — and an AI engine should be able to quote one clean sentence from it.
